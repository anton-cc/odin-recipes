Orange chicken

Orange Chicken, a beloved staple in Chinese-American cuisine, is a tantalizing symphony of sweet and savory flavors.

Succulent pieces of battered and fried chicken are coated in a glossy, citrus-infused sauce that strikes the perfect balance between sweetness and tanginess.

The dish often features zesty orange zest, garlic, and ginger, creating a harmonious blend that delights the taste buds. Orange Chicken is a crowd-pleaser, its addictive
combination of textures and tastes making it a go-to dish for those craving a delectable fusion of Asian-inspired flavors.

ingridients

    Sauce:

  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1/3 cup rice vinegar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons orange juice
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons orange juice
  • 2 tablespoons chopped green onion
  • 1 tablespoon grated orange zest
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 3 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water

    • Chicken:

  • 2 large skinless, boneless chicken breasts, cut into 1/2 inch cubes
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil

Instructions

  1. Coat chicken pieces in cornstarch, dip in beaten eggs, and fry until golden brown.
  2. In a separate pan, combine all orange sauce ingredients except cornstarch mixture.
  3. Bring the sauce to a simmer, then add the cornstarch mixture to thicken.
  4. Toss fried chicken pieces in the orange sauce until well coated.
  5. Serve over rice and garnish with green onions and sesame seeds.
